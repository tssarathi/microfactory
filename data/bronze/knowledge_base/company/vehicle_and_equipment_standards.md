# The Good Trades Co. — Vehicle and Equipment Standards

## Standard Loadout — All Trades

Every The Good Trades Co. vehicle carries:

- PPE kit: hard hat, safety glasses (clear and tinted), hi-vis vest, steel cap boots, insulated gloves, hearing protection (earmuffs and plugs)
- First aid kit (checked and restocked monthly)
- Fire extinguisher (1kg ABE dry powder, serviced annually)
- Spill kit (absorbent pads, containment bags)
- Site sign-in book
- The Good Trades Co. branded uniform — minimum 2 clean sets in vehicle at all times

## Trade-Specific Loadout

### HVAC Technician

- Refrigerant gauge set (R410A and R32 rated)
- Vacuum pump
- Refrigerant recovery unit
- Electronic refrigerant leak detector
- Anemometer (airflow measurement)
- Digital thermometer and calibrated reference thermometer
- Multimeter — CAT III rated minimum
- Capacitor tester
- Coil cleaning kit (chemical foam cleaner, pump sprayer, brushes)
- Common filters: FILT-20x20 x4 (carried as standard stock)
- Nitrogen regulator and hose for pressure testing

### Electrical Technician

- Multimeter — CAT IV rated (required for switchboard work)
- Insulation resistance tester (megger)
- RCD tester
- Voltage tester (non-contact and proving unit)
- Clamp meter
- Cable tracer
- Hand tools — insulated to AS/NZS 60900 (1000V rated)
- LOTO kit: personal padlock (red, engraved with tech ID), danger tags, lock hasp
- Labels and circuit identification markers

### Plumbing Technician

- Electric eel — 32mm (standard drains) and 50mm (main drains)
- Camera inspection unit with recording capability
- Gas leak detector (combustible gas / LEL meter)
- Pressure testing kit (hand pump, gauges, fittings)
- Pipe wrenches and cutters (assorted sizes)
- Soldering and brazing kit (for copper pipe work)
- Grease trap access equipment (or access to hired pump unit via GreenWaste QLD)

### Refrigeration Technician

All items from the HVAC loadout, PLUS:

- Gas detector rated for ammonia (for Eagle Farm and industrial ammonia sites)
- Vibration meter (for compressor baseline and trend monitoring)
- Mechanic's stethoscope (bearing noise diagnosis)
- Oil sampling kit (bottles, labels, shipping materials for lab analysis)
- Refrigerant recovery cylinders — must be certified in-date (check date stamp)
- Refrigerant log book (van-specific, records all refrigerant in/out per job)

## Tool Calibration

| Equipment | Calibration Interval | Standard |
|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| Reference thermometer | Annual | NATA-traceable calibration certificate |
| Pressure gauges | Annual | Manufacturer-specified or NATA-traceable |
| Gas detectors (LEL, ammonia) | 6-monthly | Per manufacturer schedule, bump test before each use |
| Voltage testers | Before each use | Test on known live source before and after use |
| Insulation resistance tester | Annual | Manufacturer calibration |

- Calibration due dates are tracked on the shared team spreadsheet.
- If a calibration is overdue, DO NOT use the instrument. Notify the operations manager for recalibration.
- Calibration certificates are retained for 7 years.

## Stock Management

- Common consumable parts are carried in the vehicle: capacitors, contactors, filters, fuses, cable ties, tape, sealant.
- Weekly stock check: every Monday, check van stock against the standard loadout list. Reorder anything below minimum quantity.
- Monthly van inventory audit: the operations manager conducts a physical audit of each vehicle's stock, tools, and PPE.
- Parts used on a job must be logged in the job notes (parts_request note type if ordering, or recorded in the departure note if used from van stock).

## Vehicle Standards

### Weekly Walk-Around Check (Every Monday)

- Tyres: pressure, tread depth, damage
- Lights: headlights, tail lights, indicators, brake lights
- Fluids: oil, coolant, washer fluid
- Windscreen: chips or cracks
- General cleanliness: interior and exterior

### Monthly Detailed Inspection (Logged)

- All weekly items plus:
- PPE kit condition check
- First aid kit restock
- Fire extinguisher check (pressure gauge in green zone)
- Tool condition check (damaged or worn tools flagged for replacement)
- Calibration dates reviewed
- Van signage and branding condition

### Annual

- Roadworthy inspection (QLD Safety Certificate)
- Fire extinguisher service
- First aid kit full audit and restock

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- Vehicle loadout checklist is completed every Monday morning — signed and dated.
- Missing or damaged tools must be reported to the operations manager immediately.
- All vehicles carry The Good Trades Co. branding (logo, company name, emergency contact number).
- Vehicles must be kept clean and presentable — they represent the company to every customer and road user.
- Personal items and rubbish must not be visible in the cabin when parked at a customer site.
