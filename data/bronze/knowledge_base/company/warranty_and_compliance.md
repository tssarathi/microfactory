# The Good Trades Co. — Warranty and Compliance Procedures

## Identifying Warranty Work

Before repairing or replacing any equipment component:

1. Check the equipment install date and manufacturer warranty period. This information is on the equipment nameplate or in the customer's records.
2. Review the manufacturer's warranty terms — some warranties exclude wear items, consumables, or damage caused by improper maintenance.
3. If the equipment is under warranty: DO NOT replace parts without manufacturer authorisation. Unauthorised repairs can void the warranty.
4. Document everything: photographs of the fault, all test readings, fault description, and nameplate details (model, serial, manufacture date).

## Manufacturer Warranty Claim Process

Follow these 7 steps for every warranty claim:

1. **Confirm the fault is a manufacturing defect** — not wear, misuse, or installation error. A compressor failing at 6 months is likely warranty. A filter clogging from lack of maintenance is not.
2. **Contact the manufacturer's warranty department** with:
   - Equipment model and serial number
   - Installation date
   - Fault description and diagnostic findings
   - Photographs of the fault
3. **Obtain a warranty authorisation number** before proceeding with any repair. Record this number in the job notes.
4. **Replace the defective component** with the manufacturer-supplied replacement part. Use only genuine parts for warranty work.
5. **Retain the defective part** — return it to the manufacturer if requested. Some manufacturers require the faulty part for analysis.
6. **Submit warranty claim paperwork** within 14 days of the repair. Include the authorisation number, photos, readings, and technician report.
7. **Check whether labour is covered** — some warranties cover parts only. Others cover parts and labour. Confirm with the manufacturer before quoting the customer.

### Recent Warranty Examples

- Mitsubishi MSZ-AP35VG 4-way valve failure (WO-29): warranty authorisation MW-2026-4421, parts covered, labour charged to customer.
- Fronius Symo 15.0 junction box moisture ingress (WO-37): warranty reference FRO-WAR-2026-1187, panel junction box replaced under warranty.

## Builder Defect Identification

Sometimes faults are caused by non-compliant original installation — these are builder defects, not equipment faults.

### How to Identify

- The installation does not meet the applicable Australian Standard at the time of install.
- Common examples:
  - Missing expansion control valve on mains pressure hot water system (required per AS/NZS 3500.4:2018, Clause 8.8)
  - Incorrect PTR valve rating
  - Improper ductwork connections
  - Missing or inadequate ventilation

### What to Do

1. Document the non-compliance with photographs and measurements.
2. Note which Australian Standard clause is not met.
3. Advise the customer to pursue the defect with their builder under statutory warranty.
4. In Queensland under the QBCC Home Warranty Insurance Scheme:
   - Structural defects: 6 years from completion
   - Non-structural defects: 2 years from completion
5. Provide the customer with your written findings to support their claim.
6. Do NOT repair the builder's defective work at the customer's expense unless they specifically request and approve it.

### Recent Builder Defect Example

- Rheem Stellar 330 missing expansion control valve (WO-31): PTR valve weeping due to thermal expansion. Expansion control valve required per AS/NZS 3500.4 Clause 8.8 was not installed during original build. Documented and photographed for customer's warranty claim with Metricon.

## Compliance Certificates

### When Required

| Work Type | Standard | Certificate Required |
|-----------|----------|---------------------|
| All gas work | AS/NZS 5601.1:2013 | Gas compliance certificate — mandatory after every gas installation, alteration, or repair |
| Emergency lighting testing | AS/NZS 2293.2 | Compliance certificate after annual full discharge test |
| Electrical work | AS 3000:2018 | Electrical compliance certificate for new installations and significant alterations |

### Certificate Requirements

Every compliance certificate must include:

- Date of work
- Description of work performed
- Equipment or system details (model, serial, location)
- Test results and readings
- Technician name and licence number
- Signature

### Filing

- Electronic copy uploaded to the job management system on the day of issue.
- Hard copy provided to the customer.
- The Good Trades Co. retains copies for a minimum of 7 years.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- Warranty claims are tracked in the job management system with the manufacturer's authorisation number.
- Compliance certificates are filed electronically and in hard copy.
- Annual audit of the compliance certificate register is conducted by the operations manager.
- Any warranty claim exceeding $5,000 in parts value must be approved by the operations manager before proceeding.
