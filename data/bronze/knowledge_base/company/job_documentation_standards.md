# The Good Trades Co. — Job Documentation Standards

## Note Types

Every job visit generates notes in the system. Use the correct note type for each entry:

### arrival

Log immediately on arrival at site. Include:

- Time of arrival
- Who you met or reported to (site contact name)
- Initial observations (what you can see/hear/smell before starting diagnosis)
- PPE donned for the job
- Any pre-existing damage or conditions noted (photograph these)

Example: "Arrived Eagle Farm Cold Storage. Signed in at security gate. PPE donned — hi-vis, steel caps, safety glasses. Met ops manager Craig. Freezer Hall A alarm active — temp display showing -12C, setpoint -22C."

### diagnosis

Log after completing your diagnosis. Include:

- What you found — be specific with measurements and readings
- Root cause identified or suspected
- Equipment identifiers (make, model, serial number where relevant)
- All test readings taken

Example: "Inspected Bitzer CSH8573-140Y compressor unit. Condenser fan motor seized — shaft will not rotate by hand. Bearing failure confirmed. High pressure fault — discharge pressure 28 bar (cutout set at 25 bar)."

### update

Log during the job for progress, changes, or interim measures. Include:

- What you have done so far
- Any changes to the original scope
- Delays and their cause
- Temporary or interim measures put in place

Example: "Portable cooling unit delivered and connected to Freezer Hall A. Temp stabilising at -15C — not ideal but will hold product."

### departure

Log when leaving site. This note is MANDATORY for every visit. Include:

- Summary of work completed
- Any temporary measures in place
- Next steps and expected timeline
- Customer informed of status
- Return visit scheduled (if applicable)

Example: "Departing Eagle Farm. Portable cooling running stable. Client has my mobile for overnight emergency. Returning 2026-03-14 13:00 for motor installation."

### parts_request

Log when parts are needed. Include:

- Part number and description
- Quantity required
- Urgency level (standard, urgent, emergency)
- Current stock status (in warehouse, out of stock, ordered)
- Supplier and expected delivery date if known

Example: "Need: 1x FM-300W condenser fan motor (300W 3-phase 415V 50Hz). Motor is OUT OF STOCK in warehouse — checking supplier availability. Requesting emergency order — ETA tomorrow morning."

### customer_contact

Log any communication with the customer outside the physical visit. Include:

- Method of contact (phone, email, text)
- Who you spoke with
- What was discussed
- Any decisions or commitments made

Example: "Called customer to confirm return visit Thursday afternoon. Customer confirmed they will have building access arranged. Discussed quote for additional work — customer to confirm by email."

## What to Include in Every Note

- **Be specific**: measurements, readings, part numbers, model numbers. "Compressor running hot" is not useful. "Compressor discharge temp 78C (within limits)" is useful.
- **State facts, not opinions**: "Condenser coil fouled with dust/debris reducing airflow" not "condenser is dirty."
- **Record what you did and why**: "Replaced thermostat batteries (CR2032 x2) — measured 1.8V, should be 3V."
- **Note anything unusual**: unexpected conditions, discrepancies from previous visits, potential future issues.

## Photography Requirements

Take photographs of:

1. **Before and after** for any visible work (coil cleaning, part replacement, duct repair).
2. **Equipment nameplates** for warranty claims — model number, serial number, manufacture date.
3. **Defects found** — cracked components, corroded connections, water damage, pest activity.
4. **Pre-existing damage** on arrival — protect yourself against blame for damage you did not cause.
5. **Readings on test instruments** — gauge readings, multimeter displays, thermal images.

## Readings to Record

Record ALL of the following readings where applicable:

- **Pressure readings**: suction pressure, discharge pressure (bar or kPa), with ambient temperature noted
- **Temperature readings**: supply air, return air, ambient, water temperature (degrees Celsius)
- **Electrical readings**: voltage, current draw (compare to nameplate), resistance, insulation resistance
- **Calibration readings**: before and after calibration values, reference instrument used

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- Job notes must be entered into the system on the DAY of the visit. Not the next day. Not at the end of the week.
- A departure note is mandatory for every visit — no exceptions.
- Photographs must be uploaded on the same day as the visit.
- All notes become part of the customer's permanent record. Write as if the customer, a lawyer, or a safety inspector will read it — because they might.
- Notes are never deleted or modified after submission. If a correction is needed, add a new update note referencing the original.
