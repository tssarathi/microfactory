# The Good Trades Co. — Customer Communication Standards

## First Impression

- Arrive in a clean, branded The Good Trades Co. uniform.
- Introduce yourself: "Hi, I'm [name] from The Good Trades Co., here for your [service type] appointment."
- Put on shoe covers before entering any residential property.
- Ask where the issue is and let the customer explain in their own words. Do not interrupt.

## During the Job

- Explain what you are doing in plain language. Avoid jargon — say "the fan motor has seized" not "the ECM has a locked rotor condition."
- If you discover additional issues beyond the original scope, inform the customer immediately.
- Always get verbal or written approval before performing any work outside the original scope. If additional costs are involved, provide an estimate before proceeding.
- If you need to leave site for parts, give the customer a specific estimated return time. Call if you will be delayed.

## Delivering Bad News

- Be direct but empathetic: "I've found that your compressor has failed. Here are your options."
- Explain options clearly with approximate costs:
  - Repair: cost and expected timeframe
  - Replacement: cost and expected timeframe
  - Do nothing: what happens if they choose not to act
- Give the customer time to decide. Do not pressure them into a decision on the spot.
- For warranty claims: "This appears to be covered under your manufacturer warranty. I'll need to contact [manufacturer] to arrange the claim. I'll handle the paperwork."

## Residential Customers

### Elderly or Vulnerable Customers

- Call 30 minutes before arrival so they can prepare.
- Speak clearly and at a normal pace. Do not shout.
- Be patient — allow extra time for questions and explanations.
- Offer to write down key information (what was done, follow-up needed, your contact number).

### Pets

- Before entering, ask: "Do you have any pets I should be aware of?"
- Keep external doors closed to prevent pets from escaping.
- If working in an area where a pet is present, ask the customer to secure the pet if possible.

### Apartments and Units

- Use the intercom system. State your name and company.
- Ask about parking before arriving if possible — many apartment buildings have limited or no visitor parking.
- Be mindful of noise — neighbours are close. Avoid loud work early morning or late evening.

## Commercial Customers

- Sign in at reception or security as required by the site.
- Coordinate timing with the facility manager — some sites restrict work to certain hours.
- For after-hours work, confirm access arrangements in advance (security codes, key safes, escort requirements).
- Minimise disruption to business operations. If your work will affect an occupied area (noise, power shutdown, access restriction), notify the facility manager in advance.

## Handling Complaints

1. Listen without interrupting. Let the customer express their frustration fully.
2. Acknowledge their frustration: "I understand this has been inconvenient, and I'm sorry for the disruption."
3. Focus on what you CAN do, not what you cannot: "Here's what I can do to resolve this today."
4. If you cannot resolve the complaint on-site, escalate to your supervisor with the customer present — do not leave the customer waiting for a callback that may not come.
5. Document the complaint in a customer_contact job note.

## After the Job

1. Walk the customer through what you did. Show them the work if accessible.
2. Show old parts if replaced — this builds trust and demonstrates the fault was real.
3. Explain any follow-up needed: "The part will arrive Thursday, and I'll return Friday morning to install it."
4. Leave the work area cleaner than you found it. Sweep up debris, wipe down surfaces, remove all packaging.
5. Provide relevant maintenance tips: "Cleaning the filter every month will prevent this from happening again. Let me show you how."

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- Every customer interaction represents The Good Trades Co. Professionalism is non-negotiable.
- Any customer complaint must be reported to the operations manager within 24 hours, regardless of whether it was resolved on-site.
- Follow-up call within 48 hours for all emergency jobs — confirm the issue is resolved and the customer is satisfied.
- Customer communication notes should be logged as a customer_contact note type in the job management system.
