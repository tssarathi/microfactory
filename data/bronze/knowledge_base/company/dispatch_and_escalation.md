# The Good Trades Co. — Dispatch Priorities and Escalation Procedures

## Priority Levels and Response Times

| Priority | Response Time | When to Use |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Emergency | Within 2 hours | Safety risk, critical equipment failure, product at risk (cold storage), vulnerable persons affected (elderly, infants) |
| High | Within 4 hours | Major equipment failure affecting business operations, compliance deadlines, medical facility HVAC |
| Medium | Within 24 hours | Equipment partially working, non-critical faults, scheduled follow-up visits |
| Low | Within 5 business days | Routine maintenance, minor issues, site inspections, quotes |

## Dispatch Process

Before confirming a technician for dispatch:

1. **Check availability** — review the technician schedule for the requested time.
2. **Match specialization** — ensure the technician has the right trade skills for the job (HVAC, electrical, plumbing, refrigeration).
3. **Verify certifications** — see Certification Checks below. This is a HARD GATE.
4. **Confirm parts** — check if the technician has required parts in their van, or arrange delivery/pickup before dispatch.
5. **Review site notes** — check customer record for access requirements, PPE, and site-specific hazards.

## Certification Checks Before Dispatch

These checks are MANDATORY. They are hard gates, not suggestions. Do not dispatch a technician who does not hold the required current certification.

| Certification | Required For | Verification |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| Blue Card | All work at schools and childcare facilities | Check expiry date. Expired = cannot attend. No exceptions. |
| ARCtick | Any work involving refrigerant handling | Check licence type matches scope (restricted vs full). |
| Working at Heights | Any rooftop plant access or work above 2 metres | Check expiry date. Expired = cannot work at height. |
| Confined Space | Entry to any confined space (plant rooms, pits, tanks) | Check expiry date. Expired = cannot enter. |
| Electrical Licence | All electrical work | Verify current licence with QBCC. |

If a technician's certification has expired or is expiring within 30 days, notify the operations manager for renewal action. Do not dispatch to work requiring that certification until renewed.

## Escalation Paths

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Technician cannot diagnose the fault | Contact senior technician on-call for phone guidance. If unresolved, arrange senior tech site visit. |
| Safety concern identified on-site | STOP WORK immediately. Call supervisor. Do not continue until the safety concern is resolved. |
| Customer complaint | Report to operations manager within 24 hours. Document in a customer_contact note. |
| Equipment under warranty | Do NOT repair. Contact manufacturer warranty department. Document fault with photos and readings for warranty claim. |
| Scope exceeds original quote | STOP. Contact customer. Get written approval (email acceptable) for additional work and costs before proceeding. |
| Parts out of stock | Check alternative suppliers, arrange emergency order if critical priority, deploy interim measures (portable cooling, temporary bypass), inform customer of delay and expected timeline. |

## Parts Shortage Field Procedure

If you arrive on-site and discover the required part is out of stock:

1. **Check van stock** for compatible alternatives or cross-reference parts that could substitute.
2. **Call the warehouse** for cross-reference options or substitute parts that may work.
3. **Check supplier availability** — call the preferred supplier for stock and delivery ETA.
4. **For critical jobs** (emergency or high priority): arrange emergency courier delivery or drive to the supplier for pickup. Do not leave the customer without communication.
5. **For non-critical jobs**: schedule a return visit once the part is available. Give the customer a specific expected date.
6. **Log a parts_request note** with: part number, description, quantity needed, urgency, supplier name, and expected ETA.

## After-Hours Emergency Procedure

- The on-call technician roster rotates weekly. The current roster is posted in the shared team calendar.
- On-call technician must answer the phone within 15 minutes of a call.
- For emergency priority callouts: respond on-site within 2 hours.
- If the on-call technician cannot attend (illness, prior emergency), they must immediately notify the operations manager who will arrange a backup.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- All dispatch decisions are logged in the job management system — who was dispatched, when, and why.
- Certification checks are currently performed manually by the dispatcher. Verify before confirming dispatch. This will be automated in a future system update.
- Emergency callouts must be reported to the operations manager within 1 hour of attendance, even outside business hours.
- If a technician is dispatched to a site and discovers they lack the required certification for the work (e.g., rooftop access needed but working_at_heights expired), they must NOT proceed. Contact dispatch immediately for reassignment.
