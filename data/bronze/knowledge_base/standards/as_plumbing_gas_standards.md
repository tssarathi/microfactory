# The Good Trades Co. — Australian Plumbing and Gas Standards Quick Reference

**For field use only. Not a substitute for the full standard.**
**Queensland | Updated March 2026**

---

## AS/NZS 5601.1:2013 — Gas Installations — General

**Scope:** Installation, alteration, and maintenance of gas installations using natural gas, LPG, and other fuel gases.

### Pressure Testing Procedures

- **Strength test (new or altered pipework):** Pressurize to 7 kPa (natural gas) or 10 kPa (LPG) using air or inert gas. Hold for a minimum of 10 minutes. No drop = pass.
- **Tightness test:** Conducted at normal operating pressure after the strength test. Hold for at least 3 minutes. No drop = pass.
- **Test medium:** Air or nitrogen only. Never use gas for testing. Never use oxygen.
- **Test gauge:** Must be accurate to at least 0.1 kPa.

### Gas Pressure at Appliance

- **Natural gas supply pressure:** 2.5 to 3.0 kPa measured at the appliance inlet with the appliance operating. If outside this range, adjust the regulator or investigate supply issues upstream.

### Compliance Certificates

- A **compliance certificate is mandatory after ALL gas work** — new installations, alterations, maintenance, and disconnections.
- In Queensland, submit via the QBCC online portal. Retain a copy for the customer and company records.

### Commissioning

- Purge all air from the pipework before lighting any appliance.
- Check for gas leaks using approved leak detection fluid or an electronic gas detector at every joint and connection.
- Verify correct appliance operation: ignition, flame stability, combustion analysis where applicable.

## AS/NZS 3500.4:2018 — Heated Water Supply

### Key Requirements

- **Maximum delivery temperature at sanitary fixtures:** 50°C for personal hygiene (bathrooms, showers, basins). This is a hard limit for scald prevention.
- **Tempering valves / TMVs:** Required on all heated water outlets used for personal hygiene in domestic and commercial installations. TMVs must comply with AS 4032.1 or AS 3498.
- **PTR (Pressure Temperature Relief) valve:**
  - Mandatory on all storage water heaters.
  - Must be set to operate at or below the rated working pressure and temperature of the vessel.
  - Discharge must be visible — do not pipe into a concealed location.
  - Test annually: lift the lever to confirm free operation and reseat. Replace if it does not reseat or weeps.
- **Clause 8.8 — Expansion control valve:** MANDATORY for all mains pressure storage systems. Prevents pressure build-up from thermal expansion during the heating cycle. Must discharge to a visible and safe location.

## AS 3498:2009 — Thermostatic Mixing Valves — Performance

**NOTE: The current edition is 2009, not 2019.**

### Testing and Maintenance

- **Outlet temperature stability:** The TMV must deliver water between 45°C and 50°C under normal flow conditions.
- **Response time:** The valve must react to supply temperature or pressure changes fast enough to prevent scalding.
- **Scalding protection:** If cold supply fails, the TMV must shut off hot flow within the specified response time to prevent untempered hot water reaching the outlet.

### Maintenance Schedule

| Frequency | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Quarterly | Measure outlet temperature at the nearest fixture. Record. Must be 45-50°C. |
| Annually | Full performance test: check outlet temp, response to cold supply isolation, check strainers, inspect body and internals. |
| Replacement | Replace the TMV cartridge or entire valve per manufacturer's specified service life, OR if it fails any performance test. |

## AS/NZS 3500.2:2018 — Sanitary Plumbing and Drainage

### Key Field Requirements

- **Grease traps:** Required for all commercial kitchens and food preparation areas. Sizing depends on fixture units and flow rate. Must be accessible for cleaning and maintenance.
- **Trade waste:** Any discharge that is not standard domestic sewage requires a trade waste agreement with the local water authority. Do not connect trade waste to the sewer without approval.
- **Drainage grades (minimum):**
  - 100mm pipe: 1 in 60 (1.67%)
  - 65mm pipe: 1 in 40 (2.5%)
  - 40mm pipe: 1 in 40 (2.5%)
  - Always check local authority amendments — some councils require steeper grades.
- **Backflow prevention:** Required wherever there is a risk of contaminated water being drawn back into the potable supply. Select the device based on the hazard rating (low, medium, high). Annual testing and tagging mandatory for testable devices.
