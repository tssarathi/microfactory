## AS/NZS 1677.2:1998 — Refrigerating Systems — Safety Requirements for Fixed Applications

### Refrigerant Safety Classification (CRITICAL — Know This)

| Refrigerant | Classification | Toxicity | Flammability | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|----------|--------------|-------|
| R410A | **A1** | Low | No flame propagation | Common split system refrigerant |
| R404A | **A1** | Low | No flame propagation | Commercial refrigeration |
| R32 | **A2L** | Low | **Lower flammability** | Newer split systems — NOT A1, it IS flammable |
| R717 (Ammonia) | **B2** | **Toxic** | **Flammable** | Industrial only — NOT B2L |

**R32 is A2L, not A1. R717 is B2, not B2L. Do not confuse these classifications.**

### Charge Limits

- Refrigerant charge limits vary by occupancy type and refrigerant classification.
- A2L refrigerants (R32) have lower allowable charge limits in occupied spaces than A1 refrigerants.
- Always calculate the maximum charge for the room volume before installation or system alteration.

### Pressure Testing

- **Test medium:** Dry oxygen-free nitrogen (OFN) only. Never use refrigerant, air, or oxygen for pressure testing.
- **New installations:** Pressurize to the rated test pressure. Hold for **24 hours minimum**. No drop = pass.
- **Service and repair:** Standing pressure test at operating pressure. Duration per manufacturer's specification, minimum 10 minutes. **The Good Trades Co. company policy requires a 1-hour minimum hold time for all service pressure tests.**
- **Pressure relief devices:** Required on all systems. Must be piped to atmosphere in a safe location. Never cap, plug, or disable a relief valve.

### Machinery Rooms (Ammonia / B-Class Refrigerants)

- Mechanical ventilation, gas detection, emergency shutdown, and restricted access are mandatory.
- Self-closing doors, no ignition sources, emergency signage.

## AS/NZS 4254.1:2012 — Flexible Ductwork

### Installation Requirements

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Maximum length | **6 metres** |
| Minimum bend radius | **One duct diameter** (e.g., 250mm duct = 250mm radius) |
| Connection method | **Mechanical clamp + sealant** at every collar connection |
| Support spacing | **1.5 metres maximum** between supports |
| Insulation | Must not be compressed — maintain full insulation thickness |

### Common Field Issues — Check for These on Every Service

- **Disconnected collar:** Duct has pulled off the spigot. Reconnect, clamp, and seal.
- **Crushed or kinked:** Restricts airflow, increases fan energy. Straighten the run or reroute. Replace if permanently deformed.
- **Sagging between supports:** Supports too far apart or failed. Re-support at 1.5m maximum intervals. Duct should not dip more than 50mm per metre between supports.
- **Damaged vapour barrier:** Torn or punctured outer facing allows moisture ingress into insulation. Repair with matching vapour barrier tape. Replace the duct section if damage is extensive.

## AS/NZS 1386.5 — Clean Spaces (Theatres and Controlled Environments)

### Temperature and Humidity Control

- **Operating theatres / surgery suites:** Maintain room temperature between **20°C and 22°C**.
- **Humidity control:** Where specified by the facility, maintain relative humidity within the documented range. Typically 40-60% RH for surgical environments.
- Verify and record conditions during every service visit. Report any exceedance to the facility manager immediately.

## ISO 10816 — Vibration Severity for Rotating Machinery

**NOTE: This is an ISO standard, not an Australian standard, but it is universally applied in HVAC and refrigeration service for assessing motor and compressor vibration.**

### Vibration Severity Zones

| Zone | Velocity (mm/s RMS) | Condition | Action |
|------|---------------------|-----------|--------|
| **A** | < 2.8 | New or reconditioned machinery | Acceptable — record as baseline |
| **B** | < 7.1 | Acceptable for long-term operation | Monitor — no immediate action |
| **C** | < 11.0 | Short-term operation only | Plan repair — do not leave in service long-term |
| **D** | > 11.0 | **Damage imminent** | **Shut down immediately** and investigate |

### Measurement Protocol

- **Measurement points:** Take readings in three axes — **horizontal, vertical, and axial** — on each **bearing housing**.
- **Baseline recording:** Record vibration levels on new or recently serviced equipment. This is the reference for all future trend analysis.
- **Trend monitoring:** Compare each reading to the baseline and to the previous reading. A rising trend, even within Zone B, warrants investigation before it reaches Zone C.
- **Equipment:** Use a calibrated vibration meter. Ensure the accelerometer is firmly coupled to the bearing housing — handheld probe readings are less reliable than magnetic mount.
