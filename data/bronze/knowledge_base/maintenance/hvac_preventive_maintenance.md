# The Good Trades Co. — HVAC Preventive Maintenance Schedule

Preventive maintenance checklists for HVAC systems. Print the relevant section before each service visit. Record all readings on-site and transfer to the job management system the same day.

## Condition Rating Scale

Use this scale in every maintenance report:

- **A — Excellent:** Equipment operating within all manufacturer specifications. No action required.
- **B — Good:** Minor wear or cosmetic issues. No performance impact. Note for next service.
- **C — Fair:** Maintenance required within 3 months. Schedule follow-up work order.
- **D — Poor:** Urgent attention required. Advise customer immediately. Quote for remediation.
- **F — Critical:** Do not operate. Isolate equipment. Notify customer and operations manager.

## Quarterly Service Checklist

Complete every 3 months. Allow 1-2 hours per system.

- [ ] Inspect and replace air filters. Record filter size and condition (clean/moderate/heavy soiling).
- [ ] Check thermostat calibration. Compare setpoint to measured supply air temperature. Acceptable drift: 1.0C.
- [ ] Inspect condensate drain line and trap. Flush with water to confirm free flow. Clear any algae or sludge buildup.
- [ ] Visual check of refrigerant sight glass (where fitted). Note bubble presence — persistent bubbles indicate low charge.
- [ ] Inspect electrical connections at contactor and terminal block. Look for discolouration, arcing marks, or loose terminals.
- [ ] Test safety controls: high-pressure cutout, low-pressure cutout, and anti-freeze protection. Confirm each trips and resets correctly.
- [ ] Record operating readings: suction pressure, discharge pressure, supply air temperature, return air temperature, ambient temperature, compressor current draw.
- [ ] Assign overall condition rating (A-F) and note any recommendations.

## Six-Monthly Service Checklist

Complete every 6 months. Includes all quarterly items plus:

- [ ] Evaporator coil deep clean with chemical foam cleaner. Rinse thoroughly and confirm drain is clear after cleaning.
- [ ] Condenser coil deep clean with pressure washer (outdoor units) or nitrogen purge (indoor plant rooms). Remove debris from fin surfaces.
- [ ] Torque-check all electrical connections at isolator, contactor, and terminal block.
- [ ] Measure compressor and fan motor current draw. Compare to nameplate full load amps (FLA). Flag if exceeding 90% of rated current.
- [ ] Test run and start capacitors. Record measured microfarad values. Replace if more than 10% below rated value.
- [ ] Inspect fan blades for cracks, balance, and cleanliness. Clean if buildup is visible.
- [ ] Check belt tension and condition on belt-drive supply/return fans. Adjust to manufacturer-specified deflection (typically 10mm at midpoint). Replace belts showing surface cracking.

## Annual Service Checklist

Complete every 12 months. Includes all six-monthly items plus:

- [ ] Full refrigerant system check. Record suction pressure, discharge pressure, superheat, and subcooling. Compare to manufacturer targets for current ambient conditions.
- [ ] Compressor oil analysis for systems above 20kW. Take oil sample and send to laboratory. Check for moisture, acid, and metal contamination.
- [ ] Vibration analysis on compressor and fan motors. Record axial, radial, and horizontal readings. Compare to baseline. Flag if readings enter ISO 10816 Zone C (>7.1mm/s).
- [ ] Electrical safety test: insulation resistance on compressor windings (minimum 1 MOhm at 500V DC). Record values.
- [ ] BMS point verification (where applicable). Compare BMS sensor readings to handheld instrument readings. Acceptable deviation: 0.5C for temperature, 2% for humidity.
- [ ] Asset condition assessment. Photograph nameplate, general condition, and any defects found. Assign condition rating.
- [ ] Written maintenance report with recommendations. Submit to customer within 3 business days. Retain copy in job management system.

## Equipment-Specific Procedures

### Daikin Ducted — RZQS250, ACAS140, ACAS250, FDYAN100, FDYAN140

- Filter sizes vary by model. Check nameplate or previous service records for correct dimensions. Common: FILT-20x20 panel filters.
- Static pressure check: measure at supply and return plenums using manometer. Compare to commissioning data. Acceptable range per Daikin spec: 50-150Pa external static depending on model and ductwork configuration.
- BMS integration: verify communication between indoor unit and BMS controller. Confirm all sensor readings match handheld instruments within 0.5C (ref. WO-21, WO-28 — server hall and theatre HVAC calibration work).
- EEV (electronic expansion valve): confirm stepping through range during operation. Listen for abnormal clicking or buzzing.
- Belt-drive models: check supply fan belt tension to 10mm deflection at midpoint per Daikin specification (ref. WO-21 — belt tension adjusted on server hall unit).

### Split Systems — Mitsubishi MSZ-AP25VG, MSZ-AP35VG, MSZ-AP50VG / Fujitsu ASTG24KMCA, ARTG36LHTA

- Indoor coil access: remove front panel and filters. Lift evaporator cover to access coil face. Apply chemical foam cleaner and allow to drain through condensate tray.
- Indoor fan barrel cleaning: remove fan barrel annually where buildup is visible. Soak in alkaline coil cleaner. Mitsubishi MSZ-AP series requires removal of 4 screws to access barrel. Fujitsu ASTG requires removal of drain tray first.
- Wall-mounted units: check mounting plate is secure. Confirm condensate drain hose has continuous fall with no kinks or dips.

### Carrier 42VH Series — 42VH024, 42VH036

- Filter access: slide-out panel filters behind return air grille. Check filter frame for warping.
- Heritage property considerations: these units are commonly installed in heritage-listed buildings. Do not modify existing ductwork, plenums, or penetrations without heritage approval. Record any concerns in the maintenance report and advise the customer to obtain heritage clearance before any remediation work (ref. WO-14, WO-27 — heritage building constraints noted).

### Actron ESP — ESP Plus, ESP Platinum

- Zone controller battery backup: check backup battery (CR2032) in zone controller. Replace if voltage below 2.5V. Re-pair controller to indoor unit after battery replacement if signal is lost.
- Damper motor cycling test: activate each zone individually from the controller. Confirm damper opens and closes fully. Listen for stalling or grinding. Measure airflow at each supply register to confirm damper response.
- Zone controller firmware: check current version on controller display. Note in service report.

## Reporting Requirements

All maintenance reports from The Good Trades Co. must include:

1. Condition rating (A-F) for the overall system.
2. All recorded readings (pressures, temperatures, current draws).
3. Filter sizes replaced or cleaned.
4. Any defects found with photographs.
5. Recommendations and suggested follow-up timing.
6. Refrigerant quantities added or recovered (if any), logged per Australian regulations.

Submit reports to the customer within 3 business days of the service visit.
