# The Good Trades Co. — Plumbing Preventive Maintenance Schedule

Preventive maintenance checklists for hot water systems, tempering valves, and commercial grease traps. All gas work requires a current gas fitting licence. Record all readings on-site and transfer to the job management system the same day.

## Hot Water System Annual Service

### Gas Hot Water Systems — Storage (Rheem Stellar 360, Rheem Stellar 330)

Allow 1.5-2 hours per unit.

- [ ] Gas pressure test. Measure gas pressure at the appliance test point with all other gas appliances off. Natural gas: 2.5-3.0kPa (ref. WO-19 — measured 2.75kPa, within spec). Record pressure.
- [ ] Combustion analysis. Measure flue gas composition using combustion analyser:
  - CO: must be below 200ppm in undiluted flue gas (ref. WO-19 — measured 28ppm).
  - CO2: typical range 7-9% for natural gas (ref. WO-19 — measured 8.2%).
  - O2: typical range 5-9% for natural gas (ref. WO-19 — measured 7.1%).
  - Record all values. If CO exceeds 100ppm, investigate burner condition and air supply.
- [ ] Flue gas spillage test. With the unit operating at steady state, hold a smoke match or incense stick at the flue draft diverter. Smoke must draw into the flue, not spill into the room. Spillage indicates flue blockage or inadequate combustion air supply — isolate the appliance and investigate.
- [ ] Heat exchanger flush. Connect descaling pump to service valves. Circulate descaling solution for 30-45 minutes. Flush with clean water until pH is neutral. Record scale condition (none/light/moderate/heavy).
- [ ] Clean inlet water filter. Remove, inspect, and clean the cold water inlet filter. Replace if mesh is damaged.
- [ ] Anode rod inspection (every 2 years for storage systems). Remove anode rod from top of tank. Replace if rod diameter is reduced below 10mm or if exposed core wire is visible. Record anode condition and replacement date.
- [ ] PTR (pressure and temperature relief) valve test. Lift the valve lever to verify water discharges freely through the relief drain. Release and confirm the valve reseats with no weeping. If the valve fails to reseat or continues to drip after 3 lift-and-reseat cycles, replace the valve (ref. WO-19 — PTR lifting and reseating correctly at 1000kPa).
- [ ] Check thermostat setting. Confirm storage temperature is set to 60C minimum (Legionella control) and maximum 70C.

### Electric Hot Water Systems

- [ ] Element resistance test. Measure element resistance with power isolated. Compare to manufacturer specification. Open circuit or significantly high resistance indicates failed element.
- [ ] Thermostat calibration. Measure actual water temperature at the outlet and compare to thermostat setting. Acceptable deviation: 3C.
- [ ] Anode rod inspection (every 2 years). Same procedure as gas storage systems above.
- [ ] PTR valve test. Same procedure as gas systems above.
- [ ] Check for leaks around element flange and tank fittings.

### Instantaneous Gas — Rinnai Infinity 26

Allow 1-1.5 hours per unit.

- [ ] Clean inlet water filter. Remove filter from cold water inlet. Flush under running water. Replace if mesh is torn or blocked.
- [ ] Heat exchanger flush. Connect descaling pump to service valves. Circulate descaler for 20-30 minutes. Flush with clean water. Record scale condition (ref. WO-19 — minimal scale found).
- [ ] Error code review. Access diagnostic menu on the unit controller. Record any stored error codes and clear history after noting. Common codes: 11 (ignition failure), 12 (flame failure), 14 (thermal fuse).
- [ ] Minimum flow rate test. Slowly open the nearest hot tap until the unit fires. Measure flow rate at the point of ignition using a measuring jug and stopwatch. Rinnai Infinity 26 minimum flow rate: 2.4L/min. If unit fires below this rate, operation may be unstable (ref. WO-19 — measured 2.8L/min at fluctuation point, borderline at low flow).
- [ ] PCB visual inspection. Remove front cover and inspect the printed circuit board for burn marks, swollen capacitors, or corrosion. Record any findings.
- [ ] Gas pressure test and combustion analysis. Same procedure and limits as storage gas systems above.

## TMV Testing — AS 3498:2009

### Quarterly Outlet Temperature Check

- [ ] Measure outlet temperature at a minimum of 3 representative sanitary fixtures (e.g., basin, shower, kitchen tap).
- [ ] Use a calibrated digital thermometer. Allow water to run for 30 seconds before measuring.
- [ ] Acceptable range per AS 3498: 45-50C at sanitary fixtures in healthcare, aged care, and childcare. Maximum 50C at all sanitary fixtures.
- [ ] Record temperature at each fixture. Flag any reading outside the acceptable range.

### Annual TMV Performance Test

- [ ] Full performance test on each TMV. Measure:
  - Outlet temperature stability: must remain within 2C of setpoint across the full flow range (minimum to maximum).
  - Response time: time for outlet temperature to stabilise after a sudden change in inlet conditions. Must be less than 3 seconds.
  - Scalding protection: simulate cold water supply failure. TMV must shut off or reduce flow within 3 seconds.
- [ ] Replace TMV if:
  - Temperature fluctuation exceeds 3C across the flow range (ref. WO-24 — fluctuating between 42C and 58C, replaced).
  - Response time exceeds 3 seconds (ref. WO-24 — thermostatic element sluggish, not responding within 3 seconds).
  - Visible mineral scaling on thermostatic element or valve body.
- [ ] Record all test results. Include TMV make, model, serial number, and install date.

**Hard water areas:** Where water hardness exceeds 200mg/L CaCO3, shorten TMV replacement cycle to 3-4 years (vs 5 years in soft water areas). Ref. WO-24 — premature TMV failure at 280mg/L CaCO3, replacement cycle advised as 3-4 years.

## Grease Trap Maintenance

### Monthly Inspection and Pump-Out (Commercial Kitchens)

- [ ] Inspect grease trap level. Measure grease and solids depth. Clean at 75% capacity — do not allow trap to reach 100%.
- [ ] Pump out accumulated grease and solids using vacuum truck or pump equipment.
- [ ] Inspect inlet and outlet baffles for blockage or damage.
- [ ] Check trap lid seal. Replace gasket if seal is compromised or odour is escaping.
- [ ] Record waste volume removed. Retain waste disposal receipt from licensed waste carrier.

### Quarterly Full Professional Clean

- [ ] Full pump-out of all contents.
- [ ] Scrape walls, baffles, and floor of trap. Remove all residual grease and solids.
- [ ] Flush inlet and outlet pipes with hot water to confirm free flow.
- [ ] Inspect trap body for cracks, corrosion, or structural damage. Report any defects to the customer.
- [ ] Record trap condition and note any maintenance concerns.
- [ ] Provide waste disposal receipts from licensed carrier to the customer for council compliance records.

## Reporting Requirements

All plumbing maintenance reports from The Good Trades Co. must include:

1. All recorded readings (gas pressure, combustion analysis, water temperatures, flow rates).
2. Water quality notes where relevant (hardness testing results, scale condition).
3. TMV compliance records — retained for 7 years per The Good Trades Co. policy.
4. Grease trap waste disposal receipts attached to the service report.
5. Anode rod condition and replacement history.
6. Recommendations and suggested follow-up timing.

Submit reports to the customer within 3 business days of the service visit. Issue gas compliance certificates after all gas appliance servicing per AS/NZS 5601.1:2013.
