# The Good Trades Co. — Refrigeration Preventive Maintenance Schedule

Preventive maintenance checklists for commercial refrigeration systems. Print the relevant section before each service visit. ARCtick licence required for all refrigerant handling. Record all readings on-site and transfer to the job management system the same day.

## Quarterly Service Checklist

Complete every 3 months. Allow 1.5-2 hours per system.

- [ ] Check refrigerant pressures. Record suction and discharge pressure. Compare to expected values for refrigerant type and current ambient/box temperature. Example baseline (R404A, evaporator -6C, ambient 35C): suction 3.2 bar, discharge 15.8 bar (ref. WO-17).
- [ ] Check oil level at sight glass. Oil should be visible at midpoint. Record level (low/mid/high). Clear oil with no discolouration indicates good condition.
- [ ] Test safety cutouts:
  - High-pressure cutout: verify trips at correct setpoint (e.g., 25 bar for R404A systems — ref. WO-17).
  - Low-pressure cutout: verify trips at correct setpoint (e.g., 0.5 bar — ref. WO-17).
  - Anti-freeze protection: confirm activation before evaporator reaches 0C (for medium-temp applications).
- [ ] Check defrost cycle. Confirm initiation method (timer or smart defrost) and interval are correct. Observe one defrost cycle if timing allows. Verify defrost termination temperature.
- [ ] Clean evaporator drain pan. Remove standing water, sludge, or ice buildup. Flush drain line to confirm free flow.
- [ ] Visual inspection of condenser coil. Note level of fouling (clean/light/moderate/heavy). Check fan operation and airflow direction.
- [ ] Inspect door seals on all coolroom/freezer doors. Check for tears, compression set, or gaps. Close door on a piece of paper — should require firm pull to remove. Replace seals showing daylight gaps.

## Six-Monthly Service Checklist

Complete every 6 months. Includes all quarterly items plus:

- [ ] Condenser deep clean. For clean environments: nitrogen purge or low-pressure water rinse. For kitchen environments: alkaline degreaser followed by low-pressure water rinse (ref. WO-34 — grease-fouled condenser).
- [ ] Oil quality check. Observe oil colour through sight glass. Clear = good. Dark/cloudy = moisture or acid contamination. If suspect, take sample for lab analysis.
- [ ] Vibration readings on compressor. Record axial and radial measurements at bearing housing. Compare to baseline from commissioning records. ISO 10816 zones: A (<2.8mm/s — excellent), B (<7.1mm/s — acceptable), C (<11mm/s — monitor closely). Flag if readings exceed 4.5mm/s radial (ref. WO-25 — baseline comparison methodology).
- [ ] Measure fan motor current draw. Compare to nameplate FLA. Flag if exceeding 90% rated.
- [ ] Check filter drier pressure drop. Measure inlet and outlet pressure across drier. Pressure drop exceeding 35kPa indicates a blocked drier — schedule replacement.

## Annual Service Checklist

Complete every 12 months. Includes all six-monthly items plus:

- [ ] Full refrigerant charge verification. Record superheat and subcooling under stable operating conditions. Compare to manufacturer targets. Example nominal values (R404A): superheat 6-8K, subcooling 4-6K (ref. WO-17 — superheat 7K, subcooling 5K recorded as acceptable).
- [ ] Compressor oil analysis. Take oil sample from service valve and send to laboratory for moisture, acid number, and metal content analysis. Required for all semi-hermetic and screw compressors.
- [ ] Compressor winding resistance test. Measure start, run, and common winding resistances using low-resistance ohmmeter. Compare to manufacturer specifications. Record all values.
- [ ] Safety valve inspection. Visually inspect for corrosion, damage, or evidence of discharge. Confirm correct pressure rating matches system design pressure. Do not manually lift refrigeration safety valves — replace if suspect.
- [ ] Full electrical safety test. Insulation resistance on compressor windings (minimum 1 MOhm at 500V DC). Earth continuity on all accessible metalwork. RCD test on supply circuit.

## Condenser Cleaning in Kitchen Environments

Condensers near commercial kitchens require special attention due to grease and exhaust contamination:

1. Clean with alkaline degreaser — apply to dry coil, allow 10 minutes dwell time, rinse with low-pressure water. Do not use high pressure on grease-fouled coils as it drives contamination deeper into the fin pack.
2. Install protective mesh screen over condenser intake where not already fitted (ref. WO-34 — mesh installed to reduce contamination rate).
3. Recommend monthly condenser wipe-down by kitchen staff between quarterly professional cleans (ref. WO-34 recommendation). Provide laminated instruction sheet to kitchen manager.
4. Check condenser fan capacitor at every service in kitchen environments. Grease-laden air accelerates capacitor degradation. Replace if measured value is more than 10% below rated (ref. WO-34 — capacitor tested at 4.2uF, rated 5uF, 16% low — replaced as preventive measure).

## Equipment-Specific Procedures

### Bitzer Semi-Hermetic — 4FES-5Y, 4NES-20Y, 4TES-12Y

- Oil differential pressure: check oil pump discharge pressure minus crankcase pressure. Minimum 1.5 bar differential. Low differential indicates worn oil pump or blocked oil filter.
- Valve plate inspection: accessible by removing cylinder head. Check for carbon buildup, broken reed valves, or scored seat surfaces. Inspect at annual service or if compressor efficiency has dropped (discharge pressure low for given conditions).
- Crankcase heater: confirm heater is energised whenever compressor is off. Measure heater current to confirm element is intact. Crankcase heater prevents refrigerant migration to the oil sump during off-cycles. A failed heater causes oil foaming on startup.
- Mounting bolts: check torque on all 4 compressor mounting bolts (45Nm). Inspect anti-vibration mounts for rubber deterioration (ref. WO-25 — mounts replaced when Shore A hardness reached 85 vs original spec 65).

### Bitzer Screw — CSH8573-140Y

- Slide valve operation: actuate capacity control from minimum to maximum. Confirm smooth operation with no stalling or hesitation. Record minimum and maximum capacity positions.
- Oil separator efficiency: check oil carry-over by observing oil return sight glass. Continuous oil return flow during operation indicates separator is functioning. Sluggish or absent return suggests separator element replacement.
- Capacity control verification: modulate load and confirm compressor responds to capacity demand signal. Check response time and confirm minimum unload position matches system design.
- Vi (volume ratio) adjustment: verify Vi setting matches application (medium temp vs low temp). Incorrect Vi causes inefficiency and excessive discharge temperature.

## Refrigerant Management

The Good Trades Co. requires:

- All refrigerant quantities logged per job — record grams added and grams recovered on every service visit.
- Monthly van reconciliation of refrigerant cylinder weights against job logs.
- Maintenance reports include a refrigerant usage summary for the reporting period.
- All refrigerant handling performed under valid ARCtick licence. Licence type must match refrigerant and system type.

Related completed work orders for reference: WO-17, WO-25, WO-34, WO-39.
