# The Good Trades Co. — Electrical Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) Procedure

## Regulatory References

- Work Health and Safety Regulation 2011 (QLD), Part 4.2 — Managing risks from electrical work
- AS/NZS 4836:2011 — Safe working on or near low-voltage electrical installations
- AS 3000:2018 — Wiring Rules

## Required Equipment

Every electrically-licensed technician at The Good Trades Co. is issued a LOTO kit containing:

- Personal padlock — red, engraved with technician ID number. Unique key, never shared.
- Danger tags — "DO NOT OPERATE" (red and white), pre-printed with fields for name, date, time, and reason.
- Voltage tester — CAT III rated minimum (CAT IV for switchboard work).
- Lock hasp — for multi-worker lockout situations where multiple padlocks must be applied to one isolation point.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Prepare

1. Identify ALL energy sources for the equipment you will be working on. This includes:
   - Electrical supply (single-phase, three-phase, DC)
   - Pneumatic (compressed air)
   - Hydraulic (pressurised fluid)
   - Stored energy (capacitors, springs, elevated loads)
2. Notify all affected personnel and building management that isolation is taking place.
3. Identify the specific isolation point for the equipment — confirm with circuit diagrams or labelling.

### Step 2: Shut Down

1. Shut down the equipment using normal operating controls (power button, stop switch).
2. Wait for all moving parts to come to a complete stop.
3. Allow stored energy to dissipate:
   - Capacitors: wait a minimum of 5 minutes for discharge
   - Springs: release or block
   - Hydraulic/pneumatic: bleed pressure to zero

### Step 3: Isolate

1. Open the isolation switch or circuit breaker at the identified isolation point.
2. Ensure the switch is in the OFF position and cannot drift back.
3. For plug-connected equipment: unplug and keep the plug in sight.

### Step 4: Lock and Tag

1. Apply your personal padlock to the isolation point.
   - If the isolation device cannot accept a padlock, use a lock hasp.
2. Attach a danger tag with the following information:
   - Your name
   - Date and time of isolation
   - Reason for isolation
   - Expected completion time
3. Each worker applies their OWN lock and tag. Never share or rely on someone else's lock.

### Step 5: Verify (Critical Step)

1. Attempt to restart the equipment using normal operating controls. It must NOT start.
2. Test for voltage at the work location using your rated voltage tester.
3. IMPORTANT: Test your voltage tester on a known live source BEFORE testing the isolated circuit, and again AFTER. This confirms your tester is working — a dead tester on a live circuit will kill you.
4. Confirm zero energy state at all work points before commencing any work.

### Step 6: Release (After Work is Complete)

1. Remove all tools and materials from the work area.
2. Replace all guards, covers, and panels.
3. Verify all personnel are clear of the equipment.
4. Remove YOUR lock and tag — never remove someone else's lock.
5. Restore energy and test equipment operation.
6. Notify affected personnel that the equipment is back in service.

## Multi-Worker Lockout

When multiple technicians are working on the same equipment:

- Each worker applies their own padlock to the lock hasp.
- Work cannot resume until every worker has removed their own lock.
- The last lock removed is by the worker who will restore power and test.

## Emergency LOTO Removal

Only the site supervisor can authorise emergency removal of another worker's lock. All of the following must be documented:

1. All reasonable attempts to contact the absent worker (phone, text, emergency contact).
2. A thorough check that the absent worker is not still working on or near the equipment.
3. Date, time, reason for removal, and name of the authorising supervisor.
4. The absent worker must be notified before their next shift.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- LOTO kits are issued to all technicians holding an electrical licence.
- Padlocks are red with the technician's ID number engraved — no exceptions.
- Lost padlocks must be reported to the operations manager immediately.
- LOTO procedure applies to ALL electrical work, regardless of voltage.
- Any work on switchboards requires CAT IV rated test equipment.
