# The Good Trades Co. — Refrigerant Handling and ARCtick Compliance

## Legal Requirement

Under the Ozone Protection and Synthetic Greenhouse Gas Management Act 1989, all persons who handle scheduled refrigerants in Australia must hold a current ARCtick licence issued by the Australian Refrigeration Council (ARC).

Working with refrigerants without a valid ARCtick licence is illegal. Penalties apply:

- Individual: up to $63,000
- Corporation: up to $315,000
- Intentional venting of refrigerant: additional penalties apply

## ARCtick Licence Types

| Licence Type | Scope | Required For |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| Restricted | Split systems and small self-contained units only | Residential split system service |
| Full | All refrigeration and air conditioning systems | Commercial refrigeration, ducted systems, large plant |
| Trading Authorisation | Buying, selling, and storing refrigerant | Warehouse stock management |

The Good Trades Co. requires a Full licence for all refrigeration technicians. Restricted licence holders may only work on split systems under supervision.

IMPORTANT: R32 is classified A2L (mildly flammable). It requires different handling procedures from R410A, including charge limits in occupied spaces and ignition source control. R717 ammonia is toxic and flammable — only technicians with ammonia endorsement and appropriate training may work on these systems.

## Handling Procedures

### Before Opening Any System

1. Recover all refrigerant into approved recovery cylinders before opening the system.
2. NEVER vent refrigerant to atmosphere — this is illegal and subject to penalties.
3. Use calibrated recovery equipment appropriate to the refrigerant type.
4. Record the quantity of refrigerant recovered (kg) in the job notes.

### During Service

1. If adding refrigerant, weigh the charge in — record the quantity added (kg).
2. For R32 systems: ensure no ignition sources are present during charging or recovery. Use only R32-rated recovery equipment.
3. For ammonia systems: full PPE required (ammonia-rated respirator, face shield, chemical-resistant gloves and suit). Work only in well-ventilated areas or with forced ventilation.

### After Service

1. Record all refrigerant quantities in the van refrigerant log:
   - Refrigerant type
   - Quantity recovered (kg)
   - Quantity charged (kg)
   - Net change
2. Ensure recovery cylinders are properly labelled and secured in the vehicle.

## Leak Detection

Use these methods in order:

1. **Electronic leak detector** (mandatory first check) — suitable for the refrigerant type in use. Sensitivity must be set appropriately.
2. **Soap bubble solution** (confirmation) — apply to suspected joints and connections. Bubbles confirm leak location.
3. **UV dye** (long-term monitoring) — inject into system for ongoing leak detection at future service visits.
4. **Ultrasonic detector** (large commercial systems) — for detecting leaks in noisy environments or hard-to-reach locations.

## Pressure Testing

Before recharging any system that has been opened or repaired:

- **New installations**: nitrogen pressure test, hold for 24 hours per AS/NZS 1677.2. Any pressure drop indicates a leak.
- **Service work**: nitrogen standing pressure test, minimum 1-hour hold. Record start and end pressures.
- NEVER use oxygen or compressed air for pressure testing — explosion risk.
- NEVER use refrigerant for pressure testing — wasteful and environmentally harmful.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- Every van carries a refrigerant log book. All refrigerant transactions (in and out) must be recorded per job.
- Monthly reconciliation: van refrigerant stock is audited against the log. Submitted to operations manager by the last working day of each month.
- Any discrepancy greater than 500g triggers an investigation — this may indicate an unrecorded leak or handling error.
- Recovery cylinders must be certified in-date. Expired cylinders must not be used and must be returned for recertification.
- ARCtick licence currency is checked before dispatch to any refrigeration job.
