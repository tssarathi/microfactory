# The Good Trades Co. — Site Safety Induction and PPE Requirements

## Standard PPE — All Sites, All Tasks

Every The Good Trades Co. technician must wear the following minimum PPE on every site:

| Item | Standard | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Steel cap boots | AS/NZS 2210.3 | Lace-up, ankle-height minimum |
| Safety glasses | AS/NZS 1337.1 | Clear lens for indoor, tinted for outdoor |
| Hi-vis vest | AS/NZS 4602.1 Class D/N | Day/night rated |
| Long pants | — | No shorts on any work site |

## Additional PPE by Task

| Task | Additional PPE |
|------|---------------|
| Electrical work | Insulated gloves rated to working voltage |
| Compressor rooms, plant rooms (>85dB) | Hearing protection (earmuffs or plugs) |
| Construction sites, overhead work | Hard hat (AS/NZS 1801) |
| Chemical handling, refrigerant recovery | Face shield, chemical-resistant gloves |
| Confined space entry | As per confined space entry procedure |
| Rooftop work | Harness and lanyard (see working at heights procedure) |

## Blue Card Requirements

A Blue Card (Working with Children Check) is mandatory for ALL personnel entering school or childcare sites in Queensland.

- You must carry your Blue Card and present it on request at any school or childcare site.
- The Good Trades Co. maintains a register of all Blue Card holders with expiry dates — managed by the operations manager.
- If your Blue Card has expired, you CANNOT enter a school or childcare site. Do not attempt to work around this. Notify your supervisor immediately.
- If you do not hold a Blue Card and are dispatched to a school site, notify dispatch immediately — do not attend.

Relevant sites: Coorparoo State School requires Blue Card for ALL personnel on site.

## Site Induction Procedure

On arrival at any site:

1. **Sign in** at reception, security desk, or site office. Use the site's sign-in system or The Good Trades Co. site sign-in book.
2. **Introduce yourself** to the site contact: "I'm [name] from The Good Trades Co., here for [service type]."
3. **Confirm scope of work** with the site contact before starting.
4. **Identify emergency exits and assembly points** — ask the site contact if not clearly marked.
5. **Check for site-specific hazards**:
   - Asbestos register — ask if one exists and review it before any ceiling or wall work
   - Confined spaces — identify and avoid unless you hold confined_space certification and have a permit
   - Restricted areas — ammonia plant rooms, server halls, sterile environments
   - Traffic management — forklift zones, loading docks, high-traffic areas
6. **Don additional PPE** as required by the site (some sites require hard hats, hearing protection, or specific footwear).

## Asbestos Awareness

Asbestos-containing materials (ACM) may be present in buildings constructed or renovated before 1990.

- DO NOT disturb any material you suspect may contain asbestos (fibro sheeting, pipe lagging, insulation, vinyl tiles, cement sheeting).
- If you encounter suspected ACM during your work:
  1. STOP work immediately.
  2. Do not disturb the material further.
  3. Isolate the area — prevent others from accessing it.
  4. Notify the site contact immediately.
  5. Notify The Good Trades Co. supervisor.
  6. Only a licensed asbestos removalist may handle, remove, or disturb ACM.
- Before any work in ceiling spaces or walls in older buildings, check the site's asbestos register. If no register exists, treat suspect materials as asbestos until confirmed otherwise.

Relevant sites: Coorparoo State School has an asbestos register on file — check before any ceiling work.

## First Aid

The Good Trades Co. requires first aid certification for all technicians. Currently 8 of our 10 technicians hold current first aid certification (St John Ambulance QLD).

- A first aid kit is carried in every company vehicle.
- First aid kits are checked and restocked during monthly vehicle inspections.
- Any workplace injury must be reported to the operations manager within 24 hours, no matter how minor.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- PPE kits are carried in every company vehicle and checked during monthly vehicle inspections.
- Damaged or worn PPE must be reported and replaced immediately — do not use damaged PPE.
- Blue Card register is maintained by the operations manager — check with dispatch if you are unsure of your card status before attending a school or childcare site.
- Site sign-in is mandatory for every visit, every site, no exceptions.
