# The Good Trades Co. — Emergency Gas Leak Response Procedure

## IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (First 60 Seconds)

1. DO NOT operate any electrical switches — on OR off.
2. DO NOT use mobile phones in the affected area.
3. DO NOT create any ignition source (lighters, torches, cameras with flash).
4. Open all doors and windows immediately to ventilate.
5. Evacuate all persons from the area.
6. Move to a safe distance: minimum 50 metres upwind of the suspected leak.

## Notification (From Safe Location Only)

Make all calls from at least 50 metres away from the affected area:

1. Call Emergency Services: 000
2. Call gas retailer emergency line (number on gas bill or meter panel).
3. Notify building management or property owner.
4. Notify The Good Trades Co. supervisor.

DO NOT re-enter the area until cleared by emergency services.

## For Technicians: Gas Leak Detection

### Required Equipment

- Calibrated combustible gas detector (LEL meter) — calibration must be current per manufacturer schedule (typically 6-monthly)
- Leak detection solution (soap bubble solution or commercial leak detection fluid)
- PPE: safety glasses, nitrile gloves

### LEL (Lower Explosive Limit) Thresholds

- Greater than 10% LEL: EMERGENCY — evacuate immediately, follow immediate actions above
- 5% to 10% LEL: CAUTION — increase ventilation, continuous monitoring, locate leak with detector
- Less than 5% LEL: locate and repair leak using standard procedures

### Common Leak Points

Check these locations first with your detector and leak detection solution:

- Unions and threaded connections
- Gas valve stems and packings
- Regulator vent openings
- Appliance connections and flexible hose connectors
- Meter connections
- Pipe penetrations through walls and floors

### Detection Procedure

1. Allow LEL meter to calibrate in fresh air before entering the area.
2. Start readings at floor level (natural gas is lighter than air but can pool in low areas in still conditions).
3. Systematically check all connections from the meter to each appliance.
4. Apply leak detection solution to any suspected connection — bubbles confirm leak.
5. Mark all confirmed leak locations before beginning repair.

## Post-Emergency Procedure

After emergency services have cleared the area and given approval to re-enter:

1. Conduct a full system inspection from the gas meter to every appliance.
2. Pressure test all gas lines per AS/NZS 5601.1:2013:
   - Isolate all appliances
   - Pressurise system to test pressure specified in AS/NZS 5601.1
   - Monitor for pressure drop over the required hold period
   - Any pressure drop indicates a remaining leak — locate and repair before proceeding
3. Test all appliance connections individually with leak detection solution.
4. Document everything:
   - Photographs of leak locations (before and after repair)
   - LEL readings at time of arrival and after ventilation
   - All actions taken with times
   - Parts used for repairs
5. Provide written report to:
   - Customer (hard copy or email within 24 hours)
   - Gas retailer (as required by their emergency procedures)
   - The Good Trades Co. operations manager

DO NOT restore gas supply until ALL leaks are confirmed repaired and the system passes a pressure test per AS/NZS 5601.1:2013.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- All gas-qualified technicians carry calibrated LEL meters in their vehicles.
- Gas detector calibration is tracked on the shared calibration spreadsheet — check before use.
- Any gas leak callout classified as emergency priority — 2-hour response time.
- A compliance certificate must be issued after all gas work per AS/NZS 5601.1:2013.
- Gas leak incidents must be reported to the operations manager within 1 hour of attendance.

## Reference Standards
