# The Good Trades Co. — Confined Space Entry Procedure

## Regulatory References

- Work Health and Safety Regulation 2011 (QLD), Chapter 4, Part 3
- AS 2865:2009 — Confined spaces

## Definition

A confined space is an enclosed or partially enclosed area that:

- Is not designed or intended for continuous human occupancy
- Has limited or restricted entry and exit
- Presents a risk of harmful atmosphere, engulfment, or entrapment

### Examples in Field Service

- Ammonia plant rooms (Eagle Farm Cold Storage — restricted access, ARCtick required)
- Ceiling spaces in commercial buildings
- Equipment pits and trenches
- Large ductwork and plenums
- Water storage tanks
- Grease trap chambers
- Electrical switchboard rooms with limited ventilation

## Risk Assessment

A risk assessment must be completed and documented before EVERY confined space entry. This is not a one-time document — conditions change between visits.

The assessment must identify:

- Atmospheric hazards (toxic gases, oxygen depletion/enrichment, flammable atmosphere)
- Physical hazards (engulfment, entrapment, electrical, moving parts)
- Communication requirements
- Rescue procedures
- PPE requirements specific to the space

## Atmospheric Monitoring

### Required Equipment

- 4-gas detector monitoring: O2 (oxygen), LEL (combustible gases), CO (carbon monoxide), H2S (hydrogen sulphide)
- Detector must be calibrated per manufacturer schedule and bump-tested before each use

### Safe Levels

| Gas | Safe Range | Action if Outside Range |
|-----|-----------|----------------------|
| O2 (Oxygen) | 19.5% — 23.5% | Do not enter. Ventilate and retest. |
| LEL (Combustible) | Less than 5% | Do not enter if 5% or above. Ventilate and retest. |
| CO (Carbon Monoxide) | Less than 25 ppm | Do not enter if 25 ppm or above. Ventilate and retest. |
| H2S (Hydrogen Sulphide) | Less than 10 ppm | Do not enter if 10 ppm or above. Ventilate and retest. |

### Monitoring Protocol

- Test atmosphere BEFORE entry from outside the space.
- Test at multiple levels: top, middle, and bottom of the space (gases stratify by density).
- Monitoring must be CONTINUOUS throughout the entry — do not rely on a single reading at entry.
- If any reading moves outside safe range during work, evacuate immediately.

## Entry Procedure

1. Complete confined space entry permit (The Good Trades Co. permit form).
2. Ensure standby person is positioned at the entry point — they must remain there for the ENTIRE duration of the entry. They do not enter the space under any circumstances.
3. Establish communication protocol: radio or voice check every 5 minutes minimum.
4. Ensure rescue equipment is immediately available at the entry point:
   - Mechanical retrieval system (tripod and winch) where practical
   - Rescue harness worn by entrant connected to retrieval line
   - Self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) for rescue team if atmospheric hazard present
5. For high-risk entries (ammonia, hydrogen sulphide, oxygen-depleted environments): pre-notify emergency services before entry.
6. Enter the space and commence work. Maintain continuous atmospheric monitoring.

## Rescue Procedure

- NEVER enter a confined space to rescue someone without proper equipment and training. This is the most common cause of multiple fatalities in confined spaces.
- Call 000 FIRST.
- Use the mechanical retrieval system to extract the person if possible.
- If the person cannot be retrieved mechanically, wait for emergency services with SCBA equipment.
- Administer first aid once the person is removed to fresh air.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- Confined space entry requires supervisor approval — phone approval acceptable for remote sites.
- Entry permit is valid for one shift only. A new permit is required for each entry.
- All entrants must hold a current confined_space certification.
- Confined space incidents must be reported to the operations manager immediately.
- The standby person must have a charged mobile phone and know the site address for directing emergency services.

## Site-Specific Notes

- **Eagle Farm Cold Storage**: ammonia plant room is a confined space. Entry requires ARCtick certification in addition to confined_space certification. Ammonia-specific gas detection required.
- **Murarrie Industrial Bakery**: flour dust creates explosive atmosphere risk (ATEX awareness required). LEL monitoring critical in enclosed areas.
