# The Good Trades Co. — Working at Heights Procedure

## Regulatory Reference

- Work Health and Safety Regulation 2011 (QLD), Chapter 6, Part 1
- AS/NZS 1891.1 — Industrial fall-arrest systems and devices — Harnesses and ancillary equipment
- AS/NZS 1891.4 — Industrial fall-arrest systems and devices — Selection, use, and maintenance

## Definition

Working at heights applies to any task where a person could fall 2 metres or more. This is the Queensland regulatory threshold. Common examples in field service:

- Rooftop HVAC and refrigeration plant
- High-bay warehouse lighting and ventilation
- Elevated equipment platforms and mezzanines
- Ladder work above 2 metres
- Ceiling space access where fall-through risk exists

## Hierarchy of Controls

Always work through this hierarchy — harness work is the LAST resort, not the first option:

1. **Eliminate** — can the work be done from ground level? (e.g., using extendable tools)
2. **Substitute** — can we use a safer method? (e.g., elevated work platform instead of ladder)
3. **Isolate** — can we prevent access to the fall zone? (e.g., barriers, exclusion zones)
4. **Engineering controls** — guardrails, scaffolding, fixed platforms with handrails
5. **Administrative controls** — permits, procedures, training, supervision
6. **PPE** — fall-arrest harness and lanyard as last resort only

## Harness Requirements

When a harness is required:

- Full-body harness compliant with AS/NZS 1891.1
- Inspected by the user before each use: check webbing for cuts, fraying, chemical damage; check buckles, D-rings, and stitching for wear; check lanyard and shock absorber for damage
- Annual formal inspection by a competent person — documented with date and inspector name
- Anchor points must be rated to 15kN per AS/NZS 1891.4
- Fall distance calculation: ensure sufficient clearance below the work position (harness + lanyard + shock absorber + body height + safety margin = minimum clearance needed)

## Rooftop Access Procedures

Before accessing any rooftop plant:

1. Notify building management of the planned access.
2. Check for fragile surfaces: skylights, fibre cement sheeting (fibro), deteriorated metal decking. Walk only on designated walkways where provided.
3. Establish exclusion zones at ground level below the work area — signage and barriers.
4. Weather check:
   - No work in wind speeds exceeding 40 km/h
   - No work on wet metal roofs (slip hazard)
   - No work during electrical storms
5. Confirm safe access path: fixed ladder, roof hatch, or elevated work platform.
6. Two-person rule: never work on a rooftop alone.

## Rescue Plan

A rescue plan is mandatory before any harness work begins:

- Rescue must be achievable within 20 minutes — suspension trauma (harness hang syndrome) can cause serious injury or death within 30 minutes of suspension.
- Rescue kit must be on-site and immediately accessible: rescue descent device or davit arm, first aid kit, communication device.
- All personnel on-site must be briefed on the rescue procedure before work begins.
- If the rescue plan cannot be executed with on-site resources, pre-notify emergency services (000) before commencing work.

## The Good Trades Co. Specific Requirements

- A Working at Heights Permit is required before accessing any rooftop plant. This must be:
  - Completed by the technician
  - Signed off by the supervisor (phone approval acceptable for remote sites)
  - Valid for one shift only — a new permit is required for each day
- All technicians performing rooftop work must hold a current working_at_heights certification.
- Harness equipment is inspected during monthly vehicle inspections.
- Any incident, near-miss, or equipment failure at height must be reported to the operations manager immediately.
